Game Name: Top Knights
Game Description: A top-down strategy empire builder where you pan, rotate, and zoom across a gridded war map, deploy buildings and weapons from a live quick bar, collect taxes and resources, complete quests, and defeat rival nations.

Features:
- Rotating and zoomable top-down battlefield with right-drag panning, middle-drag rotation, and wheel zoom.
- Bottom combat bar with 4 asset slots and 4 weapon slots.
- `E` assets catalog and `Q` weapons catalog, each with 50 unlockable entries.
- Drag items from the catalogs to replace quick-bar slots during combat.
- Place buildings, vehicles, units, bombs, EMP strikes, and nukes directly onto the map.
- Asset expansion now chains outward from your latest manually placed structure within about 10 tiles, while troop deployments are limited to the 8-tile frontier around your furthest placed building.
- Economy from NPC taxes, hunting animals, chopping wood, quarrying stone, and defeating enemies.
- Houses and military buildings that generate units such as villagers, warriors, archers, knights, and support troops.
- Drag-box army selection, clickable building/unit selection, a left-bottom selection icon tray, icon-click deselect, enemy hover highlighting, left-click move/attack commands, and `X` to clear placement or selection.
- Selected units now show a small eye button inside the left-bottom selection tray, letting you jump into a pseudo-3D first-person possession view from that unit's eyes with `WASD` movement, mouse look, left-click fire, right-click aim, `Shift` sprint, and `Esc` return.
- First-person mode now renders trees, rocks, buildings, units, projectiles, target health bars, floating damage, hit markers, knockback reactions, and held-weapon overlays that change between bows, crossbows, firearms, launchers, and melee weapons.
- Health, armor, projectile, splash-damage, repair, and auto-attack systems for more believable combat.
- Quest system with coin rewards and a campaign goal of conquering enemy nations.
- Enemy attacks arrive in timed waves instead of constant nonstop pressure.
- Rare weapon drops such as axes, swords, and guns can be picked up for damage bonuses.
- Locally generated PNG art for the banner, map preview, assets sheet, and weapons sheet.
- Improved top-down terrain shading, house silhouettes, unit silhouettes, and combat VFX.
- Sprite-backed rendering with locally generated top-down PNGs for 50 assets plus unit, vehicle, drop, and ability icons, all connected to world objects and quick-bar cards.
- Refined sprite art pass with more advanced top-down roofing, castle/tower detail, industrial hardware, infantry gear, and vehicle paneling across the generated PNG set.
- Added 20 new asset choices including guard barracks, ranger lodges, lancer stables, repair bays, signal beacons, tesla spires, flame towers, missile silos, supply depots, imperial mints, war foundries, observatories, storm generators, citadels, shield bastions, siege foundries, drone labs, airstrips, hover ports, and rail forts.
- Added 20 new weapon choices including pikemen, crossbow volleys, paladin charges, war wagons, fire carts, sappers, sniper teams, medic teams, flame tanks, AA halftracks, railgun tanks, siege mechs, gunships, stealth drones, assault skiffs, shield carriers, carpet bombs, orbital lasers, nano swarms, and gravity bombs.
- Catalog panels now support scrolling so the larger asset and weapon rosters stay usable during combat.
- Visuals now use richer catalog gradients, glossy card treatments, scrollbars, stronger backdrop lighting, and newly generated sprites for the expanded buildings, vehicles, aircraft, infantry, and super-weapon icons.
- The in-game UI now uses a stronger icon pass with framed item glyphs, category badges, richer catalog cards, polished quick-bar slots, and upgraded left-bottom selection cards so assets, weapons, and selections read more clearly at a glance.
- Local PNG art from the `assets` folder now overrides the generated sprite set wherever matching files exist, with the imported building, weapon, resource, and HUD art rendered about 1.2x larger for stronger battlefield/UI presence while unmatched entries still fall back safely to the built-in sprites.
- The main start menu now uses a more compact responsive layout with shorter cards/buttons and safe vertical scrolling so the lower LAN section stays reachable on shorter screens.
- Menu option for `2 Players Split Screen` with local versus mode on the same map.
- Local split-screen now also supports `3P` and `4P` versus plus `3P` and `4P` co-op, with automatic 3-way and 4-way viewport layouts and controller assignment for up to four gamepads.
- Local controller support now scales from solo pad play up to four controllers, with left-stick cursor control, right-stick camera movement, right-stick press rotation, `A` select/click, `B` weapons menu, `X` assets menu, and `Y` deselect.
- Mouse input now converts browser pointer coordinates into true canvas-space positions so hover, selection, drag-boxes, clicks, and wheel-panel targeting stay aligned even if the page is offset, resized, or displayed under browser/fullscreen scaling.
- The project now includes a GitHub Pages deployment workflow that publishes a static build of the game, while the menu automatically disables LAN room buttons on HTTPS-hosted builds and explains that LAN still requires opening the game from the built-in `http://HOST:4173` server.
- Every current building PNG in `assets/buildings` is now wired into the live asset sprite override system; where duplicate art exists for the same building type, the game uses those extra images as alternate visual variants instead of leaving them unused.
- Split-screen cameras, per-player HUDs, per-player quick bars and resources, and head-to-head win/lose states when one side loses every unit and structure.
- Expanded biome system with deserts, canyons, deadlands, rivers, marshes, forests, hills, roads, and meadows.
- Terrain now changes movement speed, tower/ranged advantage, structure productivity, build permissions, and environmental attrition for units and buildings depending on where they stand.
- Clicking an already selected quick-bar item or single selected unit a second time now deselects it.
- LAN versus support over the built-in Node server with room-code hosting and joining from localhost or a host machine's LAN IP.
- The host now runs the authoritative battlefield simulation while the guest keeps a local camera/UI and sends deploy and command inputs back to the host over lightweight room APIs.
- LAN rooms now generate a direct share link on the host's private IP, and any player opening that link can press `Start` to join the room and launch the same match for everyone already connected.
- Shared LAN links now also embed the host API origin itself, so guests use the real host machine for join/start/poll requests instead of guessing from their own local page origin.
- LAN host/join actions now also retry against the built-in `:4173` server automatically, so versus and co-op room creation still surfaces a share link even if the page was opened from a different local URL.
- The built-in LAN server now answers cross-origin preflight requests and returns LAN API responses with CORS headers, so LAN hosting from alternate local URLs can still generate a usable share link.
- The built-in LAN server now also allows private-network preflight requests, which keeps modern browsers from silently blocking LAN room creation when the page is opened from a different local origin.
- Live assist controls now include `Difficulty: Normal/Easy`, an `Esc`-triggered ceasefire that lasts about 3 in-game minutes in solo campaign, and visible speed controls for `0.5x`, `1x`, `2x`, and `5x`.
- Easy mode now softens single-player pressure by keeping enemy factions defensive, shrinking and slowing attack waves, and stopping enemy units/buildings from aggressively attacking the player.
- Added allied co-op modes for both local split-screen and LAN play, with `player1` and `player2` sharing the same side against two large enemy strongholds.
- Co-op now has PvE win/loss rules, co-op objective HUD panels, shared LAN difficulty syncing, and remote guest deployment support through the existing room-code server flow.
- Controller support now includes `LB` / `RB` zoom in addition to left-stick cursor movement, right-stick camera movement, and `R3` rotation.
- The map now carves a stronger canyon choke zone through the middle of the battlefield, with more canyon rock clutter and clearer central terrain pressure.
- Co-op enemy strongholds now open with larger garrisons and keep producing additional hostile units over time, making allied co-op battles more intense.
- Difficulty now also includes `Hard`, which adds extra hostile camps, larger/faster enemy pressure, and stronger enemy production in PvE matches.
- Terrain visuals now have richer biome shading and detail, including denser forest silhouettes, stronger canyon depth, brighter river highlights, and more readable roads.
- Environment rendering now includes stronger shoreline blending, broader biome lighting, animated water shimmer, heat-haze passes, and camera-aware atmosphere shaders layered into the battlefield.
- Adaptive music now crossfades between persistent `Calm` and `Battle` tracks based on whether active combat is actually visible inside the local camera view, and local/LAN `player2` starts its music stream from the 20-minute mark.
- Added a procedural sound-effects pass for UI clicks, panel toggles, selection, orders, deployments, harvesting, tax collection, projectile launches, impacts, explosions, pickups, quest completion, wave warnings, and victory/defeat cues.
- Trees, rocks, walls, gates, and other solid structures now behave as real battlefield boundaries that units cannot walk through.
- Combat VFX now use stronger layered explosions, smoke, impact flashes, sparks, debris shards, and death bursts for a more dramatic battlefield.
- Selected units can now be ordered onto trees and rocks to harvest them manually, breaking each resource node chunk by chunk and only earning coins plus wood/stone when a chunk is actually destroyed.
- Neutral village houses, markets, granaries, and wandering civilians can now be visited directly by selected units to collect taxes after the squad reaches them.
- Economy buildings now contribute more visible treasury income, with markets, farms, granaries, command halls, docks, refineries, lumber camps, and quarries all feeding the empire's passive economy.
- Neutral settlements now include store-like village markets and granaries, and the HUD shows live `Income` so the economic network is easier to read during play.
- Added persistent fog of war so terrain stays revealed once your units or buildings clear it, while unreached land remains hidden under the shroud.
- Added a small minimap directly below the top-left HUD that keeps the same persistent exploration reveal, plus unit/building markers and the current camera frame.
